25 February 2022

The following is a write-up of research done in the summer of 2021.

The domains of philosophy are easy to classify - they are Logic, Aesthetics, Epistemology, Ethics and Metaphysics. Would the best map of human knowledge - Wikipedia - match this?

By data scraping ~95% of Wikipedia article titles and links using the Python packages Requests and Beautiful Soup, and then plotting in Graphia as a spanning tree, I instead found the largest nodes to be Latin and History (besides the known central node of Philosophy.) To be honest, I was expecting Science to be the most connected.


12 January 2022

Is the future of VR inherently malicious? As it is, the largest userbase is in Japan where it is generally regarded as a form of escapism. Its development has mirrored previous techonology such as the iPhone (beat saber = tap tap revenge) which settled on addictive, socially-charged content consumption. We can expect, then, instagram, celebrity and twitch analogues to grow in the future; We already can capture 3D content using just a phone camera.

The current issue with VR is a lack of content. This is alarming because it means that easy-to-make addictive ideas will proliferate. And this is what we see - video games, poker and gimicks; the type that remind me of virtual flash game advertisements on the side of your browser 10 years ago. Useful software came naturally out of the invention of the computer - the only promise of VR is existential.

It is my belief, then, that the best VR ideas will be location-based exploration. To my knowledge, this has not been explored systematically, and the next best thing - YouTube - is not updated enough to keep up with hardware advancements. As it is, the quality of VR YouTube videos is roughly a quarter what it should be. Is this the case because VR has already passed its ethical inflection point? Probably not, but its beginning sure is a lot closer to the iPhone than the Mac.
